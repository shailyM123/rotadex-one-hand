# Product Pitch Video Script — Rotadex One-Hand

**Duration:** 2 minutes  
**Format:** Narrated demo with on-screen graphics  

---

## SLIDE 1: The Hook (0:00 – 0:15)

**[VISUAL]** Close-up of an elderly man's hand struggling to open a standard snap-lid pill organizer on a kitchen counter. The box slides away. Pills scatter on the floor.

**[NARRATION]**
> "Every morning, over 7 million stroke survivors in the US face an invisible battle — not with their illness, but with their pill box. Opening a simple weekly organizer with one working hand turns a 10-second task into a 5-minute struggle."

---

## SLIDE 2: The Problem (0:15 – 0:35)

**[VISUAL]** Split-screen showing: (Left) close-up of arthritic fingers struggling with a snap-lid; (Right) statistics overlaid on a hospital-themed background.

**[NARRATION]**
> "Standard pill organizers require two hands, pinch grip strength, and fine motor dexterity — the very abilities that stroke survivors and arthritis patients have lost. This leads to medication non-adherence rates as high as 50%, causing preventable hospitalizations and loss of independence."

**[ON-SCREEN TEXT]**
- 50% of stroke patients are non-adherent to medication
- 67% of elderly patients struggle with child-proof or snap-lid containers
- Over $100B annual cost attributed to medication non-adherence (US)

---

## SLIDE 3: Introducing the Solution (0:35 – 1:00)

**[VISUAL]** Dramatic reveal: The Rotadex One-Hand appears rotating slowly on a pedestal with cinematic lighting. A hand reaches in and presses the dome button with a palm.

**[NARRATION]**
> "Introducing the **Rotadex One-Hand** — a zero-electronics, purely mechanical weekly pill organizer designed from the ground up for one-handed use. No pinching. No prying. No batteries. Just one press."

**[ON-SCREEN TEXT]**
- ✓ Palm-button activation (8–12 N force)
- ✓ 7-day carousel with gravity dispensing
- ✓ Weighted non-slip base

---

## SLIDE 4: How It Works (1:00 – 1:25)

**[VISUAL]** Animated cross-section of the Rotadex showing the internal mechanism: the button pushes down the plunger, which engages the ratchet pawl, rotating the 7-compartment carousel by one click. Pills fall through the gravity chute into the scoop cup.

**[NARRATION]**
> "Here's how it works: Place your palm — or your fist, forearm, or even elbow — on the central dome and push down. A spring-loaded ratchet mechanism rotates the internal carousel by exactly one-seventh of a turn, aligning today's medication with the gravity chute. The pills slide down a smooth, curved ramp and land in the open-faced scoop cup. Just sweep them into your hand. That's it — independent medication management in under 3 seconds."

---

## SLIDE 5: Engineering Highlights (1:25 – 1:45)

**[VISUAL]** Exploded 3D CAD view (from the interactive viewer), with labels flying in for each component.

**[NARRATION]**
> "Every component is engineered for durability and accessibility. The carousel is food-grade polypropylene. The ratchet is POM acetal for wear resistance. The base has a weighted steel ring and silicone feet that grip any surface — the harder you press, the more it grips. Each compartment holds up to 10 large capsules — enough for even the most complex medication schedules."

**[ON-SCREEN TEXT]**
- 37.6 mL per compartment
- POM acetal ratchet: 100,000+ cycles
- FDA-compliant food-grade materials

---

## SLIDE 6: Impact & Call to Action (1:45 – 2:00)

**[VISUAL]** The same elderly man from Slide 1, now smiling, effortlessly pressing the Rotadex and collecting his pills in one smooth motion. Camera pulls back to show him taking his pills independently at his kitchen table.

**[NARRATION]**
> "The Rotadex One-Hand isn't just a product — it's independence. It's confidence. It's one less thing a caregiver has to worry about, and one more thing a patient can do on their own. Designed with empathy. Built with engineering. Made for real people."

**[ON-SCREEN TEXT]**
> **Rotadex One-Hand**  
> *Medication Independence, One Click at a Time.*
