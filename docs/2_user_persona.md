# User Persona & Use Scenarios

## 1. User Persona: Arthur Pendelton

```
                  ┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
                  │ Arthur Pendelton, 72                     │
                  │ Retired Postmaster                       │
                  │ Stroke Survivor (Left-side Hemiparesis)  │
                  └──────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Background
Arthur is a 72-year-old retired postmaster who lives independently in his own home. Two years ago, he suffered an ischemic stroke that left him with permanent left-side hemiparesis (weakness and partial loss of motor control on the left side of his body). He is right-handed, so his dominant hand is fully functional, but he has very limited grip and stabilization ability in his left hand. 

### Health and Medication Needs
Arthur takes several daily medications to manage blood pressure, cholesterol, and nerve pain:
- 4 pills in the morning (including one large multivitamin and one capsule)
- 2 pills in the evening

### Frustrations and Pain Points
- **Fumbling with Caps:** Standard prescription bottles are extremely frustrating to open with one hand.
- **Prying Open Lids:** Standard weekly organizers with plastic flip-top caps require Arthur to stabilize the container with his weak hand while using his strong hand to pry open the tight plastic latches. This frequently leads to the box slipping or pills spilling out onto the floor.
- **Retrieving Pills:** Once a lid is open, Arthur cannot easily tip the container (as it would spill the other days' pills). He has to use a finger to drag the pills up the vertical side of the compartment, which is difficult due to his mild arthritis.
- **Cognitive Load:** He occasionally forgets whether he has taken his morning dose, leading to anxiety and double-dosing risks.

## 2. Use Scenarios

### Scenario A: The Current Struggle (Standard Weekly Organizer)
It is 8:00 AM, and Arthur is in his kitchen trying to take his morning pills. 
1. Arthur places his standard 7-day snap-lid pill organizer on the counter.
2. He attempts to pry open the "MORN" lid for Wednesday. Because his left hand cannot hold the box down firmly, his right thumb pushes against the tab, causing the entire plastic box to slide across the smooth kitchen counter.
3. He tries again, bracing the box against the kitchen backsplash. He applies force. The snap lid suddenly pops open with a sharp click.
4. Due to the sudden release of force, the entire box jolts. Two pills from Thursday’s compartment (which was slightly loose) spill out and roll behind the microwave.
5. Frustrated, Arthur tries to scoop his Wednesday pills out of the small, deep compartment using his index finger. The pills get stuck in the sharp corners of the cell. He eventually has to pick up the entire box and tilt it, holding down the other six lids with his weak hand's fingers. It is a stressful, clumsy start to his morning.

### Scenario B: The Solution (With the Rotadex One-Hand)
Arthur is in his kitchen at 8:00 AM, using the **Rotadex One-Hand** on the counter.
1. The Rotadex sits securely on the counter. Its weighted base and non-slip rubber pad mean Arthur doesn't need to brace it with his weak hand.
2. Arthur looks at the transparent viewing window on the front. He sees the label **"WED"** highlighted in high-contrast white text, indicating it is Wednesday's dose.
3. He raises his right hand and simply presses down on the large, comfortable central dome button using his palm. The button goes down smoothly with minimal force.
4. **"Click!"** Arthur hears a satisfying mechanical click and feels a gentle tactile feedback. The internal carousel rotates by one position, aligning Wednesday's pill chamber with the gravity chute.
5. The morning pills slide down the smooth, curved chute and land in the open-faced dispenser cup at the base.
6. Arthur easily slides his index finger into the curved cup and sweeps the pills out in one smooth motion, directly into his hand. There was no pinching, no slipping of the device, and no risk of spilling other days' medications. Arthur takes his pills independently and starts his day with confidence.
