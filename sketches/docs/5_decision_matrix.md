# Morphological Chart & Decision Matrix

## 1. Morphological Chart
To generate creative solutions, the design problem of a one-handed pill organizer is decomposed into key sub-functions. The chart below displays various physical mechanisms (options) for each sub-function.

| Sub-Function | Option A | Option B | Option C | Option D |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Advancing/Selection** | Manual rotary lid | Push-button ratchet | Slide-out drawers | Piano-key levers |
| **2. Pill Containment** | Rotating single window | Flip-top hinge lids | Sliding radial doors | Open cups |
| **3. Pill Extraction** | Scoop out of deep well | Gravity feed chute | Flip-up tray tip-out | Suction cup tip-out |
| **4. Stabilization** | Heavy weighted base | Silicone suction cup | Clamp/vice mount | High-friction TPU feet |
| **5. Feedback System** | Tactile click detent | Color-coded labels | Raised Braille letters | Mechanical pointer |

---

## 2. Concept Descriptions

### Concept 1: Piano-Key Pop-up Box
- **Mechanisms:** Piano-key levers, flip-top hinge lids, deep well scoop, high-friction TPU feet, color-coded labels.
- **Description:** A linear box with 7 compartments. Pressing a large color-coded "piano key" at the front releases a latch, causing that day's spring-loaded lid to flip open. The user then scoops the pills out of the well.
- **Pros:** Simple linear layout, easy to understand.
- **Cons:** High risk of tipping because force is applied off-center. Scooping from a rectangular well is still hard with limited dexterity.

### Concept 2: Slide-out Radial Drawer
- **Mechanisms:** Slide-out drawer, sliding radial doors, gravity feed chute, silicone suction cup, tactile click.
- **Description:** A cylindrical unit with 7 small drawer drawers radiating from the center. The user pulls a loop-handle drawer outward to access pills.
- **Pros:** Separates daily compartments completely.
- **Cons:** Pulling a loop handle requires fine-finger hooking. Drawers can get stuck or be pulled out entirely, causing spills.

### Concept 3: Rotadex Indexing Carousel (Selected Design)
- **Mechanisms:** Push-button ratchet, rotating single window/gravity chute, weighted base with TPU feet, tactile click.
- **Description:** A circular device with an internal rotating carousel. Pressing a large central button indexes the carousel by $51.4^\circ$. The pills for that day align with a bottom opening and drop down a curved gravity ramp into a wide scoop cup.
- **Pros:** Zero finger-pinch required. Dynamic gravity dispensing eliminates well-scooping. High center-of-gravity stability.

## 3. Decision Matrix (Pugh Matrix)
We evaluate the three concepts against our engineering and ergonomic criteria, using a standard weekly pill box as the Baseline (scored 0).

- **Weighting Scale:** 1 (Low Importance) to 5 (Critical)
- **Scoring Scale:** -2 (Much worse than baseline), -1 (Worse), 0 (Baseline), +1 (Better), +2 (Much better)

| Evaluation Criteria | Weight | Baseline | Concept 1 (Piano-Key) | Concept 2 (Radial Drawer) | Concept 3 (Rotadex) |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| **Ease of One-Handed Actuation** | 5 | 0 | +1 | -1 | +2 |
| **Spillage Prevention (Accident Proof)** | 4 | 0 | 0 | -1 | +2 |
| **Pill Retrieval / Ergonomic Scooping** | 4 | 0 | +1 | +1 | +2 |
| **Physical Stability (Non-sliding/tipping)** | 4 | 0 | -1 | 0 | +2 |
| **Visual & Tactile Clarity** | 3 | 0 | +1 | 0 | +1 |
| **Ease of Manufacturing & Assembly** | 2 | 0 | +1 | -1 | 0 |
| **Sanitization & Cleanability** | 2 | 0 | -1 | -1 | +1 |
| **Weighted Score** | | **0** | **+13** | **-13** | **+38** |
| **Rank** | | **3** | **2** | **4** | **1** |

## 4. Score Justification and Conclusion
- **Concept 3 (Rotadex)** scored **+38**, far exceeding the other concepts.
- **Actuation:** The central button press acts along the vertical axis of the device. This pushes the base directly into the table, increasing frictional grip and eliminating the tipping moment (torque) that affects Concept 1 and Concept 2.
- **Retrieval:** Gravity dispensing eliminates the need for fingers to reach into deep slots. The pills slide out into a wide, open-faced cup, which has a smooth, curved radius that naturally guides a scooping finger.
- **Spillage:** Because only one day's medication is ever aligned with the exit slot, the user cannot accidentally drop or spill the remaining days' pills.
